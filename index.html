What is Algo Trading?

Algorithmic trading (or "Algo Trading") is a method of executing orders using automated, pre-programmed trading instructions. These instructions account for variables such as time, price, and volume to send orders to the market without manual intervention.

The Core Advantage

The human brain is prone to fatigue, fear, and greed. An algorithm executes purely on Binary Logic. If the conditions are met, the trade is taken. If not, it waits. This ensures 100% consistency across every session.

Precision & Speed

Algorithms can scan hundreds of markets and multiple timeframes simultaneously. They react to price movements in milliseconds—speeds that are physically impossible for a manual trader.

Why it matters for PointAlgo users:

Our indicators are the "Logic Layer." They provide the specific rules your algorithm needs to identify a high-probability setup before an execution occurs.

What are Quants?

A Quantitative Analyst, or "Quant," is a specialist who applies mathematical and statistical methods to financial and risk management problems. In trading, Quants use data to build models that predict the probability of market outcomes.

"The Data-First Mindset"

Probabilistic Thinking: Quants don't look for "guarantees." They look for an "Edge"—a statistical advantage over a large sample of trades.

Market Microstructure: Quants study how individual orders interact (Orderflow) to understand the true intent of institutional participants.

Signal Filtering: They use advanced math to separate market "noise" from actual trend-shifting signals.
